Old Harry Rocks

Located about 10 kilometres south of Poole and Bournemouth, these rocks are over 60 million years old (dating from the Cretaceous Period).

The erosion caused by the constant lapping of the sea means these chalk formations are ever-changing.

In fact, this erosion has taken away Old Harry's 'wife', which has been reduced to a stump visible only during low tide.

This UNESCO World Heritage site at the end of the Jurassic Coast is simply spectacular.
